Job Hunt Security Tips

Identifying Fake Job Listings

Scammers often create fake job postings to steal personal information. Look for these warning signs:

  • Requests for payment or equipment purchases
  • Poor grammar and spelling in the posting
  • Generic email addresses instead of company domains
  • Too-good-to-be-true salaries and benefits

Safe Online Applications

Protect your personal information when applying for jobs online:

  • Check for HTTPS and security indicators in browser
  • Be cautious with requests for sensitive data early in process
  • Use a dedicated email for job hunting
  • Research the company thoroughly before applying

Virtual Interview Safety

Stay safe during remote interview processes:

  • Verify the interviewer's identity and company email
  • Be wary of unusual platform requests (e.g., Wire, Telegram)
  • Don't share screens with sensitive information
  • Check your background for personal items before video calls

Background Check Warnings

Legitimate employers follow proper procedures:

  • Beware of requests for copies of IDs too early
  • Never provide bank details for "payroll setup" pre-offer
  • Research the background check company if provided
  • Real companies won't ask for your passwords
